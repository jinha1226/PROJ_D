# Claude Code Handoff: UI Help, Item Text, Skill Text, and Monster Descriptions

## Goal
PocketCrawl should explain its systems the way DCSS does: briefly, clearly, and in a way that teaches play.
These descriptions should help the player understand not only what something is, but why it matters.

This document provides text direction for:
- skills
- item categories and notable items
- essence help text
- faith help text
- monsters
- unique monsters

All text should favor clarity first, flavor second.
Short summary first, then tactical meaning.

---

## Text Style Rules

### General style
- One short identity sentence first
- One tactical sentence second
- One optional hint sentence third
- Avoid long lore walls in the default UI
- If a detailed inspect panel exists, it can include an extra line

### Structure guidelines
For skills and systems:
- What it improves
- What kind of build wants it

For items:
- What it does
- When to use it

For monsters:
- What makes it dangerous
- What helps against it

For unique monsters:
- What makes them special
- What the player should expect

## Skill Descriptions

### Melee
**Short**
- Improves accuracy and damage in close combat.

**Long**
- Melee governs fighting at arm's reach.
- It increases your chance to hit and your damage with melee weapons.
- Fighters rely on it most, but any build that expects close combat benefits from it.

### Ranged
**Short**
- Improves bows and other dedicated ranged weapons.

**Long**
- Ranged improves attacks made from a distance with ranged weapons.
- It rewards spacing and line-of-sight control.
- Rangers and cautious hybrids benefit from it most.

### Magic
**Short**
- Improves spellcasting and unlocks stronger spells.

**Long**
- Magic governs spell power and determines which spell levels you can learn and use.
- High Magic makes spells stronger, but Intelligence is still needed to learn advanced magic.
- Mages depend on it, but hybrids can use it for utility and support.

### Defense
**Short**
- Improves armor, shields, and durable front-line fighting.

**Long**
- Defense strengthens armor use, blocking, and direct survival in melee.
- It is the core defensive skill for builds that expect to trade hits.
- Fighters depend on it most.

### Agility
**Short**
- Improves evasion, mobility, and opportunistic fighting.

**Long**
- Agility improves your ability to avoid harm and exploit good positioning.
- It helps evasive builds survive without heavy armor.
- Rogues and mobile ranged builds value it most.

### Tool
**Short**
- Improves wands, thrown tools, and trick-based combat.

**Long**
- Tool governs practical combat devices such as wands and thrown utility items.
- It rewards timing, resource use, and flexible problem-solving.
- Trickery-aligned builds make the best use of it.

## Core System Help Text

### Unaware
**Short**
- An unaware enemy has not fully noticed you yet.

**Long**
- Unaware enemies are slower to respond and are vulnerable to sudden attacks.
- Ambushes, opening strikes, and tactical positioning are strongest before an enemy becomes aware.

### Will
**Short**
- Will protects against fear, confusion, sleep, and other mental effects.

**Long**
- Will is your defense against mind-affecting attacks and oppressive magical influence.
- If an enemy threatens your mind rather than your body, Will is what keeps you standing.

### Resonance
**Short**
- Resonance is a bonus created by matching compatible essences.

**Long**
- Certain essences interact when equipped together.
- These resonance effects are stronger than the sum of the parts and help define essence-based builds.

### Intelligence Requirement
**Short**
- Stronger spells require greater Intelligence to learn.

**Long**
- Magic skill determines how advanced a spell you can handle in general.
- Intelligence determines whether your mind can truly grasp that spell.
- A strong hybrid can cast useful magic, but mastery still demands a sharp mind.

## Item Category Descriptions

### Potions
**Short**
- Potions provide immediate survival, recovery, or temporary advantage.

**Long**
- Potions are consumed instantly and often save runs that would otherwise fail.
- Healing, escape, and status recovery potions should not be hoarded too greedily.

### Scrolls
**Short**
- Scrolls are single-use effects for escape, utility, and long-term growth.

**Long**
- Scrolls can solve immediate danger or improve your run over time.
- Some are tactical, some are strategic, and learning which is which is part of the game.

### Wands
**Short**
- Wands hold limited charges of focused magical power.

**Long**
- Wands are powerful but not meant for careless use.
- Their charges are precious, and the right wand at the right moment can decide a fight.

### Spellbooks
**Short**
- Spellbooks teach groups of related spells.

**Long**
- Spellbooks are one of the main ways non-mages access new magic.
- A book can open an entirely new path, but only if your mind is sharp enough to use what it offers.

### Weapons
**Short**
- Weapons define how you deal damage and what kinds of fights suit you.

**Long**
- Some weapons are fast and precise. Others are slow and crushing.
- A good weapon is more than raw damage; it shapes your whole combat rhythm.

### Armor
**Short**
- Armor trades freedom for protection.

**Long**
- Heavier armor helps you endure direct hits, but often at a cost to evasion and mobility.
- The right armor depends on whether you plan to take blows or avoid them.

### Accessories
**Short**
- Rings and amulets provide focused passive advantages.

**Long**
- Accessories are small but meaningful adjustments to a build.
- A good one may reinforce your strengths or patch a weakness.

### Essence Items
**Short**
- Essence items bind monster remnants into lasting power.

**Long**
- Essence does not behave like normal equipment.
- It changes how a run develops by granting unusual bonuses, penalties, and powerful combinations.
- Only the Essence path can truly make use of it.

## Notable Item Text Snippets

### Scroll of Enchant Weapon
- Improves the weapon you carry.
- Best used on weapons you expect to keep for a meaningful stretch of the dungeon.

### Scroll of Enchant Armor
- Improves the protection of your armor.
- Best used on armor that matches your long-term build.

### Scroll of Blink
- Repositions you a short distance in an instant.
- A strong answer to bad positioning and sudden danger.

### Scroll of Identify
- Reveals the true nature of an unknown item.
- Knowledge is often worth more than greed.

### Potion of Healing
- Restores health immediately.
- Reliable, simple, and often worth using sooner rather than later.

### Potion of Magic
- Restores magical energy.
- Most valuable when a single spell can still change the fight.

### Wand of Fire
- Burns enemies from a distance.
- Strong against soft targets and clustered danger.

### Wand of Frost
- Chills or slows enemies with focused cold.
- Useful for control as much as raw damage.

### Wand of Lightning
- Strikes fast and hard.
- Best saved for moments when raw impact matters most.

## Faith Text Pack

### War
**Short**
- Strength through steel and discipline.

**Selection**
- War favors melee, defense, and steady conquest.
- It grants stronger front-line combat and steadier survival.
- Magic grows more slowly under its banner.

**Altar**
- A blood-red standard hangs over the altar.
- Iron and old victories linger in the air.

### Arcana
**Short**
- Strength through memory, power, and spellcraft.

**Selection**
- Arcana favors magic, mana, and learning.
- It grants stronger spells and smoother magical progression.
- It offers little comfort in close combat.

**Altar**
- Blue sigils drift over a cold stone lectern.
- The air hums with unfinished words.

### Trickery
**Short**
- Strength through speed, deceit, and precise tools.

**Selection**
- Trickery favors agility, tools, ranged combat, and ambushes.
- It rewards clever positioning and flexible resources.
- It offers little help in a straight brawl.

**Altar**
- A polished mask rests beside a scatter of knives.
- The light seems to flicker around it, not upon it.

### Death
**Short**
- Strength through ruin, hunger, and the fall of others.

**Selection**
- Death favors kill-chains, draining power, and dangerous momentum.
- It rewards aggression and turns victory into sustenance.
- Ordinary comfort and healing lose some of their safety.

**Altar**
- Black wax spills over cracked stone ringed with pale bone.
- Something below answers only when the room is silent.

### Essence
**Short**
- Strength through stolen remnants and unstable transformation.

**Selection**
- Essence is a special path that replaces normal divine power.
- It allows the use of essences, stronger resonance, and highly flexible builds.
- Its strength depends on what the dungeon gives you.

**Altar**
- No idol stands here. Only a hollow basin filled with drifting light.
- Fragments of dead things turn within it like embers in water.

## Common Monster Descriptions

### Rat
- A weak scavenger, dangerous only in numbers.
- It teaches the first lesson of any dungeon: carelessness still costs blood.

### Bat
- Fast, evasive, and annoying to pin down.
- Weak on its own, but good at wasting your time and position.

### Goblin
- A common raider with little durability.
- Not dangerous alone, but often the first sign of a crowded fight.

### Kobold
- A nimble and opportunistic tunnel dweller.
- Its speed and position matter more than its strength.

### Adder
- A quick snake with venom in every bite.
- Poison resistance makes these far less frightening.

### Wolf
- A fast hunter that punishes hesitation.
- Dangerous when it reaches you before you are ready.

### Wolf Spider
- A venomous predator with speed and patience.
- Treat it as both a melee threat and a poison threat.

### Orc
- A brutal warrior who wins through direct force.
- Stronger than the smallest dungeon vermin and often backed by allies.

### Orc Warrior
- A trained front-line brute.
- Hits hard, survives longer, and forces respect in early melee.

### Deep Elf Archer
- A disciplined ranged attacker.
- Fragile up close, but dangerous if allowed to keep distance.

### Wraith
- A hungry spirit wrapped in malice.
- It is often more dangerous than its body suggests, and should not be underestimated.

### Gargoyle
- A living stone guardian with great physical resilience.
- Strong blows and magic work better than weak repeated attacks.

### Troll
- A huge brute with frightening endurance.
- If you cannot kill it cleanly, the fight quickly gets worse.

### Fire Elemental
- A being of living flame.
- Fire resistance matters, and careless positioning can turn deadly fast.

### Earth Elemental
- Slow, heavy, and difficult to bring down.
- Best answered with power or patience, not panic.

## Unique Monster Descriptions

### Ashen Magpie
- A sly scavenger touched by cinder and greed.
- Quick hands, quick feet, and a tendency to turn small mistakes into losses.
- Do not underestimate a foe that survives by picking the field clean.

### Sister Cinder
- A wandering zealot wrapped in smoke and sacred heat.
- Fire is both her weapon and her warning.
- Prepare for sustained pressure rather than a single burst.

### Viper Saint
- A venom-priest whose faith runs through fang and ritual.
- Poison is its language, and patience is its cruelty.
- Enter the fight ready to answer venom quickly.

### Stone Warden
- An ancient sentinel of weight, order, and refusal.
- It endures what would break lesser foes and grinds down reckless attackers.
- This is not a fight to rush without force to back it up.

### Harrow Knight
- A dark champion who advances with cold discipline.
- It punishes panic, overextension, and weak resolve.
- Will and positioning matter as much as raw durability here.

### Blood Duke
- A noble predator that treats battle as harvest.
- The longer its enemies fall, the stronger its presence feels.
- Winning quickly is safer than trying to outlast it.

### Storm Hierophant
- A master of wrath from above, chaining pressure into disaster.
- It turns space into danger and punishes exposed movement.
- Enter this fight with a plan, not hope.

### The Pale Scholar
- A final mind turned inward until only terrible clarity remained.
- It tests not only your strength, but your understanding of what your run has become.
- The battle should feel like a culmination, not a skirmish.

## Bestiary Hint Format Recommendation

Each bestiary entry should ideally show:
- Name
- Short description
- Threat tags
- Suggested counterplay

Example format:
- Adder
- A quick snake with venom in every bite.
- Threats: poison, speed
- Helpful defenses: poison resistance, ranged damage

Suggested threat tags:
- melee
- ranged
- caster
- poison
- cold
- fire
- will
- undead
- durable
- fast
- unique

## Implementation Recommendations For Claude Code

1. Add short and long description fields where practical:
   - skills
   - faiths
   - essences
   - monsters
   - unique monsters
   - selected items

2. Reuse this text in:
   - skill screen
   - inspect panels
   - bestiary
   - altar selection UI
   - item detail popups

3. Default to short text in compact mobile surfaces.
4. Use long text only in detail/inspect views.
5. Prioritize tactical meaning over lore if space is limited.
